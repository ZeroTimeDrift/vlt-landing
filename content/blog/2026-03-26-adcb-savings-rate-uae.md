Abu Dhabi Commercial Bank (ADCB) is one of the UAE's largest banks, particularly dominant in Abu Dhabi. A government-linked bank (Abu Dhabi government holds the majority share), ADCB has over 1 million customers and is a common choice for expats working in the capital.

But what does ADCB actually pay on savings in 2026?

---

## ADCB savings account rates (2026)

| Account | Rate |
|---|---|
| Current Account | 0% |
| ADCB Savings Account (AED) | 0.5–1.5% |
| ADCB Savings Account (USD) | 0.5–1.5% |
| ADCB Active Saver | 1.0–2.0% (monthly transactions required) |
| ADCB Touchpoints Savings | Variable (reward-linked) |
| Fixed Deposits (12 months) | 2.5–3.5% (with lock-in) |

Standard ADCB savings accounts pay 0.5–1.5% for most customers. The Active Saver account pays slightly more but requires a set number of debit card transactions per month — a condition many expats find inconvenient.

---

## The Abu Dhabi government bank premium

ADCB is part-owned by the Abu Dhabi Investment Council, which gives it access to low-cost government deposits. This means ADCB is financially stable but also has less incentive to compete aggressively on retail savings rates.

The result: strong brand, strong backing — average rates.

All major UAE banks pay under 2% on standard no-lock-in savings. This is a structural feature of the UAE banking market — high deposit margins, limited retail competition on rates.
