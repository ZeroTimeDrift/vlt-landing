## The two tiers

There's a clear split in the UAE savings market:

**Tier 1 — conditional high rates (salary routing or minimum balance required)**
These require you to route your salary to the account or maintain minimum balances. If you're an expat, freelancer, or globally mobile professional, you often can't meet these conditions — or don't want to.

- Mashreq NEO Plus: 6.25% (salary AED 10,000+/month) or 5.0% (AED 50K+ average balance)
- Wio High-Return: ~6% (salary routing)

**Tier 2 — no conditions, no lock-in**
These pay what they advertise with no requirements:
- Vault: ~5.4% current
- StashAway Simple: ~3.9%
- Wio Flexible: 3.25% AED
- Standard bank accounts: 0.5–2%

For most expats without UAE salary routing, Tier 1 products are irrelevant. The real competition is in Tier 2.

## What happened to the FAB 4%?

First Abu Dhabi Bank ran a 4% promotional rate on standard savings accounts — no salary routing required. That promotion expired March 31, 2026.

Post-promotion, FAB standard savings returned to approximately 2.5% for most customers (balances under AED 500K). This was a marketing promotion, not a sustainable rate.

This is a useful reminder: bank promotional rates have expiry dates. Vault's rate comes from a different source — institutional borrower demand in regulated lending markets — not from a limited promotional budget.

## StashAway Simple: the leading comparison

StashAway Simple is currently the best ADGM-regulated no-lock-in product for most expats. At ~3.9% projected, with no fees and UAE Pass onboarding, it's a solid option.

Vault's ~5.4% current is approximately 150bps above StashAway Simple. On AED 100,000, that's approximately AED 1,500/year more.

The difference in mechanism: StashAway Simple holds money market instruments that track the Federal Reserve rate. Vault earns from fees paid by institutional borrowers — a rate that isn't directly tied to Fed decisions.

## Sarwa Save+: declining rate

Sarwa Save+ has been declining since its 5.1% peak in June 2024. As of March 2026, it pays ~3.7% gross — or approximately 3.2% net after the 0.5% annual management fee.

The rate follows the US Federal Reserve and has declined with each rate cut. More cuts are expected in 2026.

Sarwa is ADGM + DFSA regulated, which adds credibility. But on a purely rate basis, it's now 220bps below Vault.

## Nexo: same headline, very different product

Nexo advertises up to 14% APR, but the base rate is ~5.5% as of March 2026. To earn this you need:
- $5,000+ total portfolio on Nexo
- At least $500 in the earning asset
- Ideally holding NEXO platform rewards for higher tiers

Vault's ~5.4% requires none of this — no minimum, no platform currency, no tiers.
