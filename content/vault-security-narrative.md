## 2. Email — Trust Explanation for a Skeptical User

> One of the most common questions we hear: "If Vault goes away tomorrow, what happens to my money?"
>
> The short answer: your funds are not on Vault's books. When you deposit with Vault, your money moves directly into the Sentora institutional lending market — an independent platform used by large institutional borrowers. Vault acts as the interface; it does not hold or control your funds. The ~5.4% earnings rate reflects fees paid by those borrowers, not a return Vault manufactures or guarantees. The rate is variable and will move over time. You can withdraw at any time.
>
> We are also in the process of obtaining ADGM (Abu Dhabi Global Market) regulatory approval. In the meantime, the non-custodial structure means your funds are not exposed to Vault's operational risk in the way they would be with a conventional bank or fintech that holds deposits.

---

## 3. Key Protection Messaging Pillars (4–5 bullets)

- **Non-custodial.** Vault does not hold your money. Your funds go directly into institutional lending markets — they are not on Vault's balance sheet and not at risk if Vault experiences operational issues.

- **Institutional-grade lending markets.** Your money earns from fees paid by institutional borrowers — banks, funds, and large financial entities — through the Sentora lending market. These are not consumer peer-to-peer loans.

- **Transparent earnings source.** The ~5.4% rate comes from borrower fees, not from Vault subsidising a headline number. What you earn is what the market generates. It is variable, not guaranteed.

- **Withdrawal any time.** There are no lock-ins. You can withdraw your full balance whenever you need it.

- **Pursuing ADGM regulation.** Vault is in the process of obtaining regulatory approval from the Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM) Financial Services Regulatory Authority. We will update users as this progresses.
